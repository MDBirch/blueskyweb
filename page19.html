Copyright Constraints, and the Law


(1.5)


It is important to stay within the copyright laws. Images that have a watermark or are signed by the artist cannot be used on this website, with express written consent from the producer of that image. The copyright, Designs and Patents Act of 1988 is the current UK copyright law. Copyright arises when an individual or organisation creates a work, and applies to a work if it regarded as original, and exhibits a degree of labour, skill or judgement (reference). Copyright law covers Literacy (e.g. documents, computer programs and newsletters), Dramatic (e.g. Plays), Music, Artistic (images and logos), sound recordings and films. There are many resources on the internet where free artwork, images and fonts were available, so this was not too much of a constraint on the making of this project. It is also important to reference any information or quotes taken from other articles or academic research.
