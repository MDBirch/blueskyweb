Review of Outcomes Matching Requirements


(2.5)


Through use of my Trello board i could set out the individual items required for the project. Once they were implemented or completed, they could then be moved to the 'Done' section to confirm the outcomes met the requirements of the brief. For example, as the written work was completed for each section of the website, they would be ticked off on the list and moved to the 'Done' section. When certain parts of the HTML and CSS coding were integrated they would also be put in the 'Done' section, for example the use of a 'Navbar' for users to find sections of the website easily. Often new tasks that were required were added in the 'To Do' part of the board, as new problems or areas of improvement arose throughout the project. For example, when improving the aesthetics, i would set a new task to look at new fonts, backgrounds and images. Also as new needs arose, i would sometimes have to improve my knowledge of HTML and CSS. To check if the new elements of the project were fit for purpose, they were regularly tested, and the final version of the website was tested on different web browsers and devices to make sure everything worked.
